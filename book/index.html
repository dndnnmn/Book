As i've read this Chapter it really made things interesting because it sets up the whole story of the book, i've also notice how it's trying to portray the harsh social and political climate of Spanish rule in the Philippines. This Chapter has even made more interested in reading the book, it sets up the character of Ibarra being unbothered about any bad energy from others, especially Padre Damaso. Instead he was more focused with reconnecting with his fellow people. This chapter made me want to know more about Ibarra.

This chapter really pulled me in. Ibarras energy is just something else here hes so unaffected by all the drama and, honestly, Padre Damasos shade doesnt even faze him. Instead of getting riled up, hes all about reconnecting with everyone. Its like hes there to catch up, not to clash. This chapter just makes me want to dig deeper into what Ibarras all about.

This Chapter was intense, because those who were at higher positions view Ibarras action as dangerous even tho he just wants to make the society better by building schools and etc. This shows that people that wants to reform society are viewed as dangerous peoples by those people that manages the traditional systems.

Chapter 5 (A Star in the Dark Night)

This Chapter amazed me because Ibarra faces a lot of problems, he really wanted to make change for the good of society but is held back by people who is deeply rooted in tradition and corruption. It shows Ibarra as a star in the midst of darkness and corrupt system. It's like a star trying to shed light to it's power but then darkness is just too powerful.

Chapter 6 (Captian Tiago)

This Chapter focuses on a man named, "Capitan Tiago". Tiago is a man with great wealth and power, he is also associated with the church and local government. Capitan Tiago's character shows how power and money can blur the lines between right and wrong, and how people use their resources to manipulate their way into society's good graces.
